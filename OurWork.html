What do we do?

WWF now addresses the global threats and forces that impact species and landscapes in addition to saving species and landscapes. In response to the increasingly complex and urgent challenges facing our planet, we have refined our approach around an ambitious new strategy. People are at the center of our new strategy, which is organized around six key themes: forests, marine, freshwater, wildlife, food, and climate change. Our unique assets can be better leveraged and all our resources can be directed to protecting vulnerable places, species, and communities worldwide when these six areas are linked together. In order to preserve nature's ability to provide for all living things, including ourselves, we must put people at the center of our work.

Our Ambition

There has been a change in the world. Within a generation, wildlife numbers have halved. There is an increasing pressure on natural habitats. We are entering uncharted territory due to climate change. There has been a change in people as well. There is a greater awareness of threats to essentials such as food, water, and clean air. There is a greater sense of ambition, a greater sense of innovation. The number of corporations backing them is growing.
